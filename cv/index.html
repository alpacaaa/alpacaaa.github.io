About me

I never have enough of this stuff, seriously.

Even if I started as a backend developer, I got very much interested in user experience, which I strongly believe should be put above anything else.
My involvement in a project usually spans multiple areas, I enjoy wearing different hats and put myself in uncomfortable situations (we're still talking programming languages, right?).

Working in a team is invaluable to me because having different points of views benefit both the project and your personal growth.

I've been an enthusiastic Symphony CMS developer and fond of its content first approach.
I've attended the two community meetups held in 2010 and 2011 and get to know some really interesting people. I still occasionally use Symphony when freelancing, but it's a bit sad that the project isn't pushing forward anymore.

Overall, being part of that community has been a great experience!

Stuff I know

I'm quite proficient with Symfony2, but I consider it bloated and a pain to debug. I know Javascript well enough and it's my language of choice nowadays, although I have extensive experience with PHP. Learning node.js better is on my todo list, as I've used it only for smallish projects with socket.io and redis.

Over the past months, I've been developing with Ember.js and enjoying it a lot. A stable release has been around for some time now and it feels pretty solid indeed!

My knowledge of HTML5 and CSS3 is fairly good, I strive for clean and semantic markup and try hard to make the content look good on any device with responsive design.
I love coffeescript (hated it for a long time though) and SASS. Build tools are a truly godsend — I've used Grunt and Brunch.

A year ago I barely knew what testing was all about. I haven't embraced any methodology so far (BDD looks great) and I still don't believe a 100% test coverage is something worth aiming for. That being said, I write unit and integration tests more often than not now.

I consider a good (and fast) deployment process a must because it saved my bacon countless times.
